Pricing & Service Tiers

HOA-MX is designed to be adopted in stages. Start with the essentials for compliance and documentation, and grow into a full operations and professional-services stack as your community needs evolve.

Minimal

For small or self-managed boards that primarily need clear records, compliant notices, and simple communication tracking.

Basic

For associations that want day-to-day operational tools: billing, maintenance, Telegram communications, and standard reports.

Premium

For communities that want a full digital office: software, operations, and structured support from accounting, legal, and vendor partners.

Three ways to run your HOA with HOA-MX

Each tier builds on the previous one. Minimal focuses on compliance and transparency. Basic adds operational tools. Premium layers in coordinated professional services.

Minimal

Focus: Records & Compliance

Best for: Smaller, self-managed boards starting to standardize documentation and communication.

  • Telegram communications archive (mail, email, voicemail, SMS)
  • Document Vault for bylaws, budgets, and insurance
  • Board Governance basics (motions, meetings, minutes)
  • Core Directory for residents, board, and vendors
  • Standard Reports & Audits (annual summary exports)

Outcome: A clean, searchable history of decisions, notices, and records that supports board continuity and Minnesota CIC record-keeping expectations.

Basic

Focus: Operational Efficiency

Best for: Boards that want to centralize billing, maintenance, and day-to-day communication with residents and vendors.

  • Everything in Minimal, plus:
  • Billing & Payments (dues, assessments, fines)
  • Maintenance & Work Orders (resident → manager → vendor)
  • Enhanced Directory & Contact Center with call routing
  • More detailed Reports (payments, violations, maintenance spend)

Outcome: Fewer lost messages, clearer workflows, and a single system for tracking what was requested, when it was done, and how it was paid for.

Premium

Focus: Full Governance & Professional Support

Best for: Associations that want the HOA-MX platform plus structured help from accounting, legal, and risk partners.

  • Everything in Basic, plus coordinated access to:
  • Accounting & Treasury workflows (budgets, reserves, reconciliation)
  • Legal & Compliance support (bylaw review, meet & confer logs)
  • Insurance & Risk management (renewals, claims documentation)
  • Vendor Oversight tools (contracts, COIs, performance logs)
  • Board Training & Transition resources (“digital binder”)

Outcome: A digital-first management model that keeps the board in control while experts handle specialized tasks in a structured, documented way.

Included in every HOA-MX plan

Unified communications

Document-centered governance

Bylaws, budgets, insurance policies, and key notices live in the Document Vault, tied back to meetings, motions, and Telegram entries.

Board continuity

As board members rotate, HOA-MX preserves the institutional memory: who decided what, when, and based on which documents or communications.

Professional services as add-ons

Annual professional services can be attached to Basic or Premium tiers, or engaged separately as the board’s needs change.

Accounting & Treasury

Budget preparation, reserve scheduling, bank reconciliation, and year-end reporting. Designed to align with Minnesota HOA financial expectations.

Legal & Compliance

Insurance & Vendor Oversight

How pricing is scoped

HOA-MX pricing is based on a few simple factors: number of units, number of active vendors, and whether you need software only or software plus annual services. No per-resident logins and no surprise fees.

1. Understand your community

We look at unit count, number of board/manager users, and the mix of vendors (landscape, snow, maintenance, insurance, legal).

2. Choose your tier

Decide whether Minimal, Basic, or Premium best fits your current needs. You can always move up as your board’s workload changes.

3. Add services if needed

Attach accounting, legal, insurance, or training services as add-ons. Each service is quoted per association, not per user.
