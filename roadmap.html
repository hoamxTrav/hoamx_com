HOA-MX Roadmap

HOA-MX is being built in phases from foundation and compliance to operational intelligence and analytics. This roadmap outlines what each phase delivers for boards, residents, and vendors.

2026: Foundation & Platform

Finalize schema, align with Minnesota CIC law, and ship the core platform across billing, maintenance, Telegram, governance, and the document vault.

2026: Services & Operations

Automate accounting, insurance, vendor oversight, board training, and property-specific modules like trees, grounds, and rentals.

2027+: Pilot & Intelligence

Validate with real associations, then layer on summaries, predictive budgeting, and integrations for long-term governance.

Phase 1 — Foundation & Compliance (Q1 2026)

Finalize the structural, legal, and data backbone of HOA-MX so every later feature sits on a compliant, well-structured base.

Finalize data schema

Confirm fields in the HOA-MX schema for billing, maintenance, communications, directory, and governance so all modules share a single source of truth.

Regulatory alignment

Implement Minnesota Senate File 1750 changes (notice rules, fine limits, meet-and-confer) and §515B.3-118 record-keeping timelines directly into platform logic.

Document Vault & OCR

Stand up the first OCR pipeline for bylaws, insurance policies, and budgets, feeding the Document Vault with searchable text.

Database layer

Deploy the PostgreSQL schema with role-based access, encryption, and a clear separation between HOAs as tenants.

Design finalization

Apply Brand Book V3.1: Terracotta and Natural Oak palette, text contrast rules, and accessibility guidelines.

Board & legal templates

Standardize board governance documents – motions, votes, and compliance logs – so every HOA starts with strong defaults.

Phase 2 — Core Platform Build (Q2 2026)

Deliver the working MVP across the seven core product offerings and run the first live pilot.

Billing & Payments

Build ACH and manual payment flows, ledger reconciliation, and unit-level history tied directly to the Document Vault and Telegram.

Maintenance & Work Orders

Resident → manager → vendor workflow with photos, estimates, status changes, and satisfaction feedback.

Telegram communications

Unified ingestion of mail, email, voicemail, SMS, and chat into a single, searchable communication log.

Directory & Contact Center

Resident, board, and vendor roles with call recording and voicemail transcription, tied to Telegram threads.

Reports & Audits

SQL-based reporting layer and annual summary export (PDF) for payments, violations, and maintenance.

Board Governance

Digital motion and vote tools with compliance tagging for meet-and- confer and other S.F. 1750 requirements.

Document Vault integration

Link documents to Telegram entries and governance events so every decision has supporting evidence.

First pilot

Launch the platform with Rice Lake Terrace (rltta.hoamx.com) to validate workflows, UX, and data structures.

Phase 3 — Professional Service Automation (Q3 2026)

Build tools that support annual professional services: accounting, insurance, vendor oversight, training, and legal/compliance.

Accounting & Treasury

Automated reconciliation, reserve forecasting, and budget views tied directly to the billing and payment modules.

Insurance & Risk

Renewal tracking and mapping of homeowner vs. master policy claim responsibility, with supporting documents.

Vendor Oversight

Contract expiry reminders, proof-of-insurance logging, and vendor performance snapshots.

Board Training Portal

“Digital Binder” with video and document orientation for new board members and officers.

Legal & Compliance Dashboard

Track amendments, conflicts of interest, meet-and-confer logs, and other compliance events in one view.

Audit Mode

One-click generation of bundles for board minutes, financials, and key communications for audits or disputes.

Phase 4 — Operational Intelligence (Q4 2026)

Integrate property-specific and environmental management modules – the physical reality under the financials and communications.

Tree Management

GPS, photos, health status, and treatment records for individual trees, linked to vendor work orders.

Grounds & Snow

Schedulers, photo evidence capture, and vendor rating dashboards for mowing, trimming, and snow removal.

Landscaping & ARC

Design requests, digital approvals, and homeowner liability forms for improvements and special projects.

Rental & Insurance Tracking

Lease uploads, renewal dates, and insurance-compliance checks for rental units.

Restricted Device Compliance

Signed liability acknowledgments and inspection schedules for higher-risk items like wood-burning fireplaces.

Telegram Intelligence Layer

Semantic tagging of communications into themes such as finance, maintenance, and legal for better reporting.

Phase 5 — Pilot Launch & Expansion (Q1 2027)

Validate the full platform across multiple associations and finalize the service model for scale.

Pilot sites

Run HOA-MX with Rice Lake Terrace and 2–3 additional HOAs representing small, medium, and large tiers.

Training & support

Certify call center operators and digital admins to handle Telegram, work orders, and governance flows.

Governance feedback loop

Collect resident and board surveys, analyze Telegram patterns, and refine workflows.

Brand alignment

Deploy the final logo, color themes, and typography from Brand Book V3.1 across pilots.

Marketing & scale-up plan

Publish the 3-tier plan (Minimal / Basic / Premium) and prepare documentation for white-label or franchise deployment.

Phase 6 — Intelligence & Analytics (2027 → 2028)

Move from structured data capture to predictive governance and automated assistance.

AI summary layer

Automatically generate meeting minutes, resident letters, and vendor pattern summaries from Telegram and document data.

Predictive maintenance & budgeting

Use historical work orders and costs to forecast budgets, reserve needs, and vendor performance trends.

Open Public Lounge (OPL)

Emoji-based resident engagement board with low-pressure, moderated channels for events, ARC, and board topics.

Integrations

Connect to accounting APIs, insurance partners, and municipal reporting so data flows automatically where it needs to go.

Guiding principles

Every phase of the roadmap is anchored in a few non-negotiable principles.

Transparency first

Every message and document should be traceable, searchable, and tied back to decisions and spending.

Human-centered automation

Automation reduces workload and confusion; people still make the important calls and maintain relationships.

Compliance anchored

Minnesota CIC law is built into the product design, not treated as an afterthought or optional add-on.

Accessible by design

Readable fonts, strong contrast, and mobile-first layouts so residents of all ages can use the system.

Scalable architecture

Each HOA runs as a modular tenant instance on the same platform, allowing growth without losing control.

Documented evolution

Changes to workflows, law, and branding are recorded so future boards can see how the system evolved.
