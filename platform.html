Core Modules

These are the everyday HOAMX tools for boards, managers, and support staff. Think of them as the digital office that keeps money, work, and communication aligned.

Billing & Payments

Collect dues, special assessments, and fines via ACH, debit, or mailed checks. Every unit has a clear, searchable payment history and automatic ledger updates.

Optional late-fee rules follow board policy and Minnesota limits on fines and notice requirements.

Maintenance & Work Orders

Residents can submit requests by web, phone, or mail. Board members or managers assign vendors, attach photos and estimates, and track completion notes.

Residents see status updates and can provide satisfaction feedback when work is completed.

Communications “Telegram”

Telegram centralizes all inbound and outbound communication— letters, email, voicemail, and meetings—into a single, searchable record.

Supported formats include PDF (letters), MP3 (voicemail), HTML (email), MP4 (video), and TXT (transcripts).

Board Governance

Track motions, digital votes, and meetings in one place. Agenda templates and optional e-signatures help boards standardize their process.

Built to reflect the new Minnesota “meet and confer” and notice expectations so decisions are documented and defensible.

Document Vault

Store and categorize bylaws, insurance policies, budgets, and key notices. The vault provides a simple, permanent archive that supports board continuity and transparency.

Directory & Contact Center

A unified directory for residents, board members, and vendors, with phone routing and voicemail transcription.

Optional “call center” mode adds recording and dictation storage for HOAs that want a live operator.

Reports & Audits

Generate summaries of payments, violations, and maintenance spend to support annual meetings and audits.

Reporting is designed to help boards comply with document and recordkeeping obligations in Minnesota CIC law.

Annual Professional Services

When boards want more than software, HOAMX can connect them to professional services that plug directly into the platform: accounting, legal, insurance, vendor oversight, tax, training, and reserve strategy.

Accounting & Treasury Administration

Budget preparation, reserve planning, monthly bank reconciliation, expense categorization, year-end reporting, and vendor 1099/W-9 tracking.

Escrow and operating accounts can be managed with dual-signature controls.

Legal & Compliance Support

Annual review of governing documents, drafting of meet-and-confer policies, conflict-of-interest disclosures, and vendor contract templates that reflect Minnesota HOA law.

Insurance & Risk Management

Support for master policy and D&O renewals, comparative quoting, and coordination of certificates and claims with vendors and owners.

Vendor Oversight & Contract Administration

RFP and renewal cycles, performance documentation, inspection logs, proof-of-insurance tracking, and annual W-9/COI updates.

Recordkeeping & Archival Compliance

Digital indexing of minutes, budgets, and letters; secure backups; and annual audits of Telegram-style communications for completeness.

Tax Preparation & Audit Coordination

Support for IRS forms (1120-H or 1120), CPA compilation or review, and coordination if a formal audit is required.

Board Training & Transition Services

Orientation on budgets, meetings, and compliance, plus officer transition support and updates to the digital binder (bylaws, insurance, contacts).

Resident Communications & Printing

Annual mailings (budget notices, meeting invites) and transcription of voice or mailed content into Telegram so mixed-age communities can stay in sync.

Reserve Investment & Money Management

HOAMX offers optional reserve investment support for boards seeking yields higher than traditional CDs while remaining firmly within their fiduciary and statutory responsibilities. The focus is capital preservation, liquidity, and documentation.

Compliant reserve strategy

Work with low-risk, capital-preserving instruments such as FDIC/NCUA-insured accounts, U.S. Treasuries, and government-only money market funds—aligned with Minnesota 515B reserve requirements and your governing documents.

Treasury ladders & cash flow

Design 3–12-month Treasury ladders based on your reserve study and planned projects, so upcoming roof, siding, or paving work is funded without locking money away too long.

Clear boundaries on risk

Define which investments are off-limits for the association (e.g., equities, crypto, speculative products) so the board has a written, enforceable line between prudent reserve management and inappropriate risk-taking.

Brokerage & custodian support

Integrate with custodians such as Schwab or Fidelity so reserves stay in the association’s name. HOAMX provides templates for resolutions, signers, and trading authority.

Integrated reporting

Monthly or quarterly portfolio summaries can sync with HOAMX accounting tools so reserve balances, ladder positions, and interest income appear in the same place as operating results.

Sample reserve blueprint

For boards that want a starting point, HOAMX can propose a diversified reserve blueprint (for example, a blend of short-term Treasuries, Treasury money market, and insured deposits) tailored to your time horizon and risk tolerance.

Important: HOAMX does not act as a broker-dealer or investment advisor. We provide structure, documentation, and coordination so your board and its chosen financial professionals can implement a compliant reserve strategy.

Operational Layer

Beyond software and services, HOAMX supports the real-world operations boards deal with every season: trees, grounds, snow, landscaping, architectural changes, rentals, insurance responsibilities, and restricted devices.

Tree Management

Track location, photos, health, treatments, and cost history for each tree in the community. Useful for both safety and long-term budgeting.

Grounds Keeping

Document mowing and trimming schedules, inspection photos, and resident requests (for example, missed areas or debris cleanup).

Snow Removal

Record snowfall start/stop, service response times, type of response (plow, shovel, salt), and vehicles that block service.

Landscaping Projects

Track home and common-area landscaping with plant types, materials, before/after photos, and vendor approvals.

Special ARC Requests

Manage architectural change requests with homeowner responsibilities, rule references, timelines, inspections, and digital acknowledgments.

Rental Record Keeping

Keep leases, approvals, statuses (approved, pending, violation), and associated fines organized, with links to payment and insurance records.

Insurance Tracking

Map claims, costs, and deductibles across the master policy and individual homeowner responsibilities, so the board can see where risk lives.

Restricted Device Compliance

For items like woodburning fireplaces, track signed legal acceptance, proof of additional insurance, inspection intervals, and reminders.
